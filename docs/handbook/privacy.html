Privacy Policy

Graywolf APRS — effective 2026-05-21

Graywolf APRS is a single-station amateur radio application. It runs entirely on your own device (desktop, Raspberry Pi, or Android tablet). The developer does not operate any service that collects data from your installation. This page describes what Graywolf APRS does with your data, what stays on your device, and what is transmitted on the network or over the air when you enable specific features.

Information Graywolf APRS does not collect

Graywolf APRS contains no analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting, no advertising identifiers, and no developer-side account system. The developer receives no information about your use of the application, including but not limited to: how often you launch it, what features you use, your callsign, your location, your channel configuration, or any error or diagnostic data. There is no "create an account" flow and no cloud sync.

Information that stays on your device

The application stores the following data locally on the device where it is installed. None of it is transmitted to the developer or to any third party except as you explicitly configure (see below):

  • Your station callsign and configured channels, beacons, digipeater paths, iGate filters, and other operator settings.
  • Logs of received and transmitted packets, when you have a packet log enabled.
  • Position history from your GPS device, when you have the position log enabled.
  • Message history for APRS messaging, when you use the messaging feature.
  • Cached map tiles, when you view the live map.
  • A randomly generated per-launch authentication token on Android (used only by the in-app web view to talk to the local server; not persisted across launches).

Network connections you may initiate

Graywolf APRS only contacts external services when you enable the corresponding feature. None of these are on by default; you configure them deliberately.

  • APRS-IS: if you configure the iGate or outbound beacon submission, the application connects to an APRS-IS server you specify and transmits your callsign, your position (if you have a position beacon), and any packets you are gating to the internet. APRS-IS is a public network operated by the amateur radio community; data sent to it is publicly visible.
  • OpenStreetMap tiles (default): the application ships with OpenStreetMap as the default tile source. When you use the live map, raster tiles are fetched directly from the public OpenStreetMap tile servers. OSM sees your IP address and the coordinates of tiles you request. See openstreetmap.org's privacy policy.
  • Graywolf Maps (optional, opt-in): you may switch the tile source to "Graywolf Maps", a vector-tile service operated by the developer at maps.nw5w.com. Switching to this source is opt-in and requires a one-time registration step.
    • What is collected at registration: when you register, the application sends only your amateur radio callsign (uppercased, with any -SSID stripped) to the developer-operated registration endpoint at auth.nw5w.com/register. No email address, no name, no location, no device identifier, and no other data is sent.
    • What you receive in return: a per-callsign access token. The token is stored locally on your device and is included as an Authorization: Bearer header on every tile request to maps.nw5w.com.
    • How the callsign is used: the registration server associates the issued token with your callsign so that tile-server requests can be attributed to a licensed amateur operator for abuse prevention and per-callsign rate limiting. The callsign and the token issuance time are retained on the server for the lifetime of the registration. The tile server logs request metadata (callsign, IP, tile coordinates, timestamps) for the purpose of operating the service and detecting abuse; logs are not shared with third parties.
    • How to remove your registration: contact the address at the bottom of this page. Or, simply switch the tile source back to OpenStreetMap; the token stays on your device but is no longer used.
  • Remote Actions (optional, opt-in): the Remote Actions feature lets you trigger actions on remote graywolf stations via APRS messages authenticated by one-time passwords. If you enable this feature, the per-remote credentials you configure are stored locally on your device; nothing is sent to any developer-operated service for this feature.
  • Update checks (desktop only): the desktop version periodically checks the developer's GitHub repository for a newer release. The Android version does not perform update checks; the Play Store handles updates.

Information transmitted over the air

As an amateur radio application, Graywolf APRS transmits radio packets that include your licensed callsign whenever you have transmission enabled. Amateur radio transmissions are public by regulation: they can be received by anyone with appropriate equipment, and your callsign and any data you transmit (including position, messages, and weather data) are not confidential. This is a property of amateur radio itself, not of this application.

Hardware permissions

On Android, the application requests the following hardware permissions. Each is used solely for the in-app function described; none of the data leaves the device through the application:

  • Microphone (RECORD_AUDIO): required to receive and decode audio from your radio for packet decoding. Audio is processed in memory by the modem and is not recorded to disk or transmitted.
  • Location (ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION): required only if you enable GPS-based beaconing. Location data is used to populate your beacon position and is stored only in your local position log; it is transmitted only as part of the APRS beacons you configure to send.
  • Bluetooth (BLUETOOTH_CONNECT): required only if you use a Bluetooth-connected KISS TNC. Used only to communicate with paired TNC hardware.
  • USB device access: required only if you use a USB-connected radio interface (Digirig, AIOC, CM108, CP2102, and similar). Used only to communicate with the attached interface.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): used to show the foreground service notification that Android requires while the radio is active.

Children

Graywolf APRS is designed for use by licensed amateur radio operators. Amateur radio licensing is regulated by the country of operation and is not generally available to children under the licensing age. The application is not directed at children under 13 and is rated for ages 18 and up on the Google Play Store.

Data retention and deletion

Because no data is collected by the developer, there is no developer-side retention period and no deletion request to submit. To delete the local data on your device, uninstall the application. To delete data from external services you have opted into (APRS-IS, map tile providers, auth.nw5w.com), refer to the privacy policy of those services directly.

Changes to this policy

This policy is part of the Graywolf APRS source code and is published at chrissnell.com/software/graywolf/privacy.html. Material changes will be reflected here and the effective date at the top of the page will be updated. Historical versions are visible in the project's public git history.
